üéØ Why Set Revenue Goals?

"A goal without a plan is just a wish." Setting a clear annual revenue goal gives you direction, helps you make decisions, and provides a benchmark for measuring success.

Breaking it down into monthly and weekly targets makes the big number feel achievable and helps you course-correct early when you're falling behind.

üßÆ Setting Your Goal

Start with what you need, not what sounds impressive

Consider these factors when setting your goal:

üìä Tracking Methods

üìà Cumulative Tracking

Compare total YTD revenue against where you should be. Best for seeing overall progress.

üìÖ Monthly Tracking

Compare each month to target. Best for identifying trends and seasonality.

üí° Pro Tips

  • Set a stretch goal AND a minimum. Your stretch goal motivates; your minimum ensures survival.
  • Account for seasonality. Not every month will be equal ‚Äî plan for slow seasons.
  • Review monthly, not daily. Revenue fluctuates; monthly trends matter more than daily noise.
  • Break it down by client. Know exactly how many clients at what rate you need.
  • Celebrate milestones. Hitting 25%, 50%, 75% of goal is worth recognizing.
  • Adjust mid-year if needed. Goals should be ambitious but achievable ‚Äî adjust if circumstances change.
